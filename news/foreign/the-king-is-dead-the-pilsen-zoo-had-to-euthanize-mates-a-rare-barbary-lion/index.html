The king is dead. The Pilsen Zoo had to euthanize Mates, a rare Barbary lion

By Amber Kite
Published  March 10, 2025 11:23am EST
Dallas
FOX 4

Share

  • Copy Link
  • Email
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • LinkedIn
  • Reddit
article

Lev Mates has lived in the Pilsen Zoo since 2012

The Brief

    • Mates, a rare 15-year-old Barbary lion at Pilsen Zoo, was euthanized on February 27 due to severe health deterioration.
    • An autopsy revealed irreversible lung and heart issues, confirming the necessity of the decision.
    • Since 2012, Mates fathered 15 cubs, spreading his lineage across multiple European zoos.

Pilsen Zoo - Mates, a fifteen-year-old male rare Barbary lion from the Pilsen Zoo, is dead. After consulting with veterinarians, the keepers had him put to sleep on Thursday, February 27.

"On Wednesday, his health suddenly deteriorated significantly and he again showed his previous breathing difficulties," said the zoo's spokesman Martin Vobruba. "During Thursday morning's check-up, he was already showing apathy. After consultation with the veterinarian, we proceeded to euthanasia. The subsequent autopsy revealed irreversible changes in the lungs and heart," Vobruna said.

Mates was born in the Olomouc Zoo in 2010. "He has lived in Pilsen since 2012 and with two females he has fathered a total of fifteen young since 2016," Vobruba recalled, elaborating: "Nine of them now live in eight different zoos in four European countries and some already have their own offspring. Its genes are thus passed on to the next generations."
